Genesis AI has evolved from a one-time brand package product into a multi-layer recurring revenue business.

**Layer 1 — Entry: Brand Packages (one-time)**
- Essential: $229 — full digital brand kit
- Premium: $349 — + hard copy brand book
- Ultimate: $849 — + full printed stationery pack

**Layer 2 — Retention: Subscription (monthly)**
- Starter: $39/mo — consultant access, limited asset regenerations
- Growth: $79/mo — unlimited consultant + unlimited assets + social media templates, carousels, post graphics
- Pro: $149/mo — everything + AI-managed social media calendar

Social media assets (carousels, posts, stories, graphics) are part of the subscription — not a separate product. AI managing a brand's social account is the premium future tier.

**Layer 3 — Expansion: Physical Products**
- Business cards, banners, roller banners, t-shirts, mugs, stationery
- Ordered in-platform; fulfilled by print-on-demand partners (Printful/Printify/Gelato style)
- Zero inventory risk; ~20–40% margin
- Natural extension: once you have a brand, you want branded things

**Layer 4 — Growth: AI Social Media Management (future premium)**
- AI manages the brand's social media presence end-to-end
- Generates, schedules, and publishes content autonomously
- Brand voice and visual identity always consistent
- This is a distinct premium tier, not yet built

**Why this matters:**
Original model (alpha era) was a one-time PDF brand book transaction. LTV was the package price. The subscription + physical products model changes LTV from ~$349 one-time to potentially $349 + $79×12+ per customer — a 3–6× improvement on the same acquisition cost.

**How to apply:** When discussing pricing, revenue, or product roadmap, use this four-layer framing. The subscription with social assets is the retention engine. Physical products are the zero-risk expansion layer. Don't treat it as just a brand generator.
